# Translation Bible

The project's **long-term memory** (Wu et al. 2024, §3.2.1). Single source of truth for proper nouns, place names, dates, technical terms, cultural references, and style decisions.

This file is authoritative. If a past translation contradicts the bible, fix the past translation — not the bible — unless the bible itself is wrong.

The `translation-bible` skill auto-maintains this file during the loop. You shouldn't normally need to edit by hand; entries arrive as the agents encounter new proper nouns.

## Decisions log

Record decisions about contested translations so future passes don't re-litigate.

- 2026-04-26 — Decided to render `先生` as `the Master` (capitalized) consistently throughout the *Chuanxi lu*, rather than alternating with "Wang Yangming," because the disciples' record-keeping voice depends on the title. Footnote on first chapter occurrence identifies the referent.
- 2026-04-26 — Decided to render `良知` as `innate knowing` with `liangzhi` parenthetical on first chapter occurrence, following Bryan Van Norden's contemporary usage rather than Wing-tsit Chan's "innate knowledge of the good," because "innate knowing" preserves the verbal force of *zhi*. Avoided "moral intuition" — too Western.
- 2026-04-26 — Decided to render `格物` as `rectifying things` in Wang's voice, and `investigating things` in passages glossing Zhu Xi, with footnote on first chapter occurrence explaining the polemical disagreement. Wang himself glosses `格 = 正` ("rectify"), so this honors his stated meaning.
- 2026-04-26 — Decided to render `心即理` as `the mind is principle` (not "mind-as-principle" or "principle is mind") because Wang's polemic against Zhu Xi requires the predicative copular form to land. Italicize on first occurrence per chapter.
- 2026-04-26 — Decided to keep `qi` as `qi` (transliteration) rather than "vital energy" or "material force," with one-time gloss; the term recurs too often for a long English equivalent to remain readable. Established usage in contemporary translations (Van Norden, Slingerland).
- 2026-04-26 — Decided that `親民` will be rendered `loving the people` and `新民` as `renewing the people`, italicized when introduced as the textual disagreement in §1, because the entire opening dialogue turns on the Wang-vs-Zhu reading of these two characters in the *Great Learning*.

## Style decisions

- Transliteration system: Hanyu Pinyin without tone marks (default; Wade-Giles permitted for figures whose names have established English forms, e.g., Confucius, Mencius, Lao Tzu)
- Name order: Surname-given for Chinese names (e.g., Zhuangzi, Wang Yangming); given-surname for Western names
- Honorifics: drop generic prefixes (lao-, xiao-); keep philosophically loaded titles (zi 子, fuzi 夫子) with first-occurrence gloss
- Philosophical terms: render core terms (e.g., 道, 仁, 氣) with a chosen English equivalent on first use, then keep pinyin (dao, ren, qi) thereafter — bible captures the chosen pairing per term
- Measurements: convert li/jin/etc. to imperial in narrative; preserve original with bracketed conversion in directly quoted classical text
- Citations: classical citations follow Chicago author-date when in scholarly notes; in-line attributions in the body use the canonical English title (e.g., "Analects 4.15") with pinyin transliteration on first appearance
